This week,
the House Benghazi Select Committee held our second public hearing on efforts
to secure U.S. diplomatic facilities.
We
should not, as some have suggested, move on until there is a complete
understanding of why requests for additional security were denied, by whom they
were denied, and why an Ambassador trusted to represent us in a dangerous
country was not trusted when he asked for more security.
Jonathan Gruber, an MIT professor, helped the Administration design the Affordable Care Act (ACA). He visited the White House
21 times
and according to press reports, the Administration paid him
$400,000
for his services. Mr. Gruber also made headlines when it was revealed he
claimed , on numerous occasions, the Administration could pass the ACA thanks only to the stupidity of the American voter and a lack of transparency. We had the opportunity to ask him about these remarks at a House Oversight Committee hearing this week:
