Lawmakers push bill to end sex bias in medical studies
Reuters / June 17, 2014 / By Will Dunham
Biomedical research backed by the U.S. National Institutes of Health
must use both male and female lab animals, tissues and cells under a
bill introduced in Congress on Tuesday aimed at stamping out a bias
toward males in studies not involving human subjects.
Tennessee, led by Lipscomb, stands out in report on best teacher
training
The Tennessean / June 17, 2014 / By Joey Garrison
Tennessee, led by Nashville's Lipscomb University and a new online
school, is a silver lining in an otherwise scathing new report on the
quality and effectiveness of higher education teacher training programs
in the United States.
Human trafficking is target of TBI, TDOT campaign
The Tennessean / June 17, 2014 / Maranda Faris
The Tennessee Bureau of Investigation and Tennessee Department of
Transportation have rolled out a new campaign this week to combat human
trafficking in the state.
Metro Archives moving to downtown library
The Tennessean / June 17, 2014 / By Tony Gonzalez
The Metro Archives and its five million historical documents will soon
be housed at the downtown Nashville library, completing a move out of
Green Hills this month.
AmeriCorps funds help Nashville nonprofits
The Tennessean / June 17, 2014 / By Casey Harper
As thousands of Nashville teenagers slept away their Tuesday mornings
and summer breaks, 50 rambunctious kids milled around outside the Martha
O'Bryan Center at 7:30 a.m., eagerly waiting for its doors to open.
Iraqi premier Maliki gaining strength as Shiites rally behind him
Washington Post / June 17, 2014 / By Liz Sly
BAGHDAD - Iraqi Prime Minister Nouri al-Maliki is tightening his hold on
power in response to the catastrophe unfolding in Iraq, even as his
critics blame his policies for causing the mayhem that is tearing the
country apart.
Extremists Said to Take Control of Iraq's Biggest Oil Refinery
The New York Times / June 18, 2014 / By Rod Nordland
BAGHDAD - Iraq's biggest oil refinery fell to Islamic extremists
Wednesday morning, after army helicopter gunships failed to beat back an
attack on the facility 130 miles north of Baghdad, according to refinery
workers, eyewitnesses and an Iraqi army officer who fled the scene.
Ukrainian Leader Proposes a Unilateral Cease-Fire in the East
The New York Times / June 18, 2014 / By David M Herszenhorn
MOSCOW - President Petro O. Poroshenko of Ukraine proposed on Wednesday
a unilateral ceasefire by government forces as part of a 14-point peace
plan to end the separatist violence in the eastern part of the country.
As China's Leader Fights Graft, His Relatives Shed Assets
The New York Times / June 17, 2014 / By Michael Forsythe
HONG KONG - As President Xi Jinping of China prepares to tackle what may
be the biggest cases of official corruption in more than six decades of
Communist Party rule, new evidence suggests that he has been pushing his
own family to sell hundreds of millions of dollars in investments,
reducing his own political vulnerability.
