Van Hollen Discusses Budget Deal on Morning News
Congressman Van Hollen recently appeared
on Fox 5 Morning News to discuss the recent bipartisan budget deal that
will help replace the job-killing sequester without hurting working
families, including hundreds of thousands of hardworking federal
employees.

Click here to watch the video >>
Federal Employees Health Benefits Forums Recap
Congressman Van Hollen hosted
his 11th Annual Forum on Federal Employees Health Benefits in
cooperation with the National Active and Retired Federal Employees
Association (NARFE) in three locations throughout Maryland's Eighth
Congressional District -- in Wheaton, Rockville, and Mount Airy. A
panel of experts answered questions, and more than 15 insurance
providers were on hand to meet individually with attendees. Hundreds of
constituents attended these forums and were able to gather the
information they needed to make informed choices on their options.
Congressman Van Hollen looks forward to hosting these forums again next
year.
For more information on the FEHB Program, visit OPM.gov >>
Maryland Department of Veterans Affairs Service Program Opens New
Office
The Maryland Department of Veterans Affairs Service Program has opened
a new service center at Montgomery College, 51 Mannakee Street,
Counseling and Advising Building, Room CB 103, Rockville, MD. Marlon
Brown, the Montgomery County Veterans Benefits Specialist, will also
hold walk-in office hours the first and third Thursdays of each month
starting in January at the Department of Labor, Licensing and
Regulation office in Wheaton (11002 Viers Mill Road, Ste. 100, Wheaton,
MD 20902). Mr. Brown can be reached at 240-567-4505 or by email at
vbsmbrown@gmail.com.
For more information, visit maryland.va.gov >>
Maryland Extends Deadline to Enroll in Health Coverage
All health insurance carriers have agreed to allow Maryland to extend
the enrollment deadline from December 23 to December 27. This means
that Marylanders who enroll in a Qualified Health Plan through Maryland
Health Connection
by December 27 can have coverage that begins on
January 1; consumers would have until January 15 to pay for their
coverage. Medical carriers participating in Maryland Health Connection
include CareFirst, Kaiser Permanente, United Healthcare and Evergreen
Health Co-Op.
