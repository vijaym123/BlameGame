Mayor Karl Dean announced plans Thursday for an online encyclopedia of
Nashville history and culture, with nine community meetings starting
next week to seek ideas from local residents.

U.S. to Send Up to 300 Military Advisers to Iraq
The New York Times / June 19, 2014 / By Mark Landler and Michael R.
Gordon
WASHINGTON - President Obama said Thursday that he would deploy up to
300 military advisers to Iraq to help its struggling security forces
fend off a wave of Sunni militants who have overrun large parts of the
country, edging the United States back into a conflict that Mr. Obama
once thought he had left behind.
Refugees at Levels Not Seen Since World War II
The New York Times / June 20, 2014 / By Nick Cumming-Bruce
GENEVA - The number of people displaced by violent conflict hit the
highest level since World War II at the end of 2013, the head of the
United Nations refugee agency, Antnio Guterres, said in a report
released on Friday, warning that "peace is dangerously in deficit."
Rebels seize former chemical weapons plant; Sistani urges formation of
new government
Washington Post / June 20, 2014 / By Liz Sly and Loveday Morris
BAGHDAD - Islamist insurgents continued to bear down on Iraqi forces
Friday, seizing a former chemical weapons facility and battling for
control of the country's largest oil refinery, as the spiritual leader
of Iraq's Shiite Muslim majority called for the formation of a new
"effective" government with broad support.

commander in the Iraqi Army. So did the tiny cup from which he was
drinking his tea, the ashtray where he snuffed out his cigarettes and
the Ping-Pong table and treadmill outside his office.

Uber Isn't Worth $17 Billion
FiveThirtyEight / June 18, 2014
/By Aswath Damodaran
Earlier this month, investors poured $1.2 billion into Uber, a tech
company whose smartphone app connects taxi drivers to passengers. The

worth $17 billion, a mind-boggling sum for a young company with only a
few hundred million dollars in revenue.
