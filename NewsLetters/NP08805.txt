Nashville songwriter Lee Thomas Miller heads to Congress
The Tennessean / June 9, 2014 / By Nate Rau
Nashville songwriter Lee Thomas Miller will be representing his fellow
professionals in front of Congress on Tuesday at the House Judiciary
subcommittee hearing on music licensing.
A-Z builds business by helping veterans
The Tennessean June 9, 2014 / By Shelley DuBois
Someone has to get veterans the medical equipment they need. Nashville
entrepreneurs Dinah and John Norman have built a business out of
supplying vets with medical devices - everything from a cane to a
wheelchair - as quickly as possible.
Legal Aid gets grant to expand free lawyer help
The Tennessean / June 5, 2014
/By Tony Gonzalez
Free legal assistance for the poor could reach further in Middle
Tennessee because of a new $50,000 grant given to the Legal Aid Society
of Middle Tennessee and the Cumberlands.
Too Few Trucks Puts Nashville's Fire Department in a 'Life or Death
Situation'
WPLN / June 9, 2014 / By Bobby Allyn
Over the next year, Nashville will be running short of firetrucks. The
city has an aging fleet that's in an out of repair. While the city is in
the process of ordering more, firefighters could be scrambling in the
meantime.
Wall Street Journal recommends a long weekend in Nashville
Nashville Business Journal / June 9, 2014 / By Brandon Gee
The Wall Street Journal spent a long weekend in Nashville, and dozens of
local businesses are benefitting from a shout out as a result.
$1B mixed-use development planned near Natchez Trace
The Tennessean / June 8, 2014 / By Getahn Ward
Nashville developer John Rochford has 850 acres surrounded by the
Natchez Trace Parkway under contract, with plans for a development that
would cost more than $1 billion and be built over the next two decades.
Gunmen attack Karachi airport; Pakistani Taliban claims responsibility
Washington Post / June 9, 2014 / By Tim Craig
ISLAMABAD, Pakistan - Heavily armed gunmen who attacked Karachi's
international airport planned to destroy airplanes and possibly take
hostages, Pakistani government and security officials said Monday.
Jeff Miller: Sloan Gibson doing 'great job' at VA
Politico / June 9, 2014 / By Jonathan Topaz
House Veterans' Affairs Committee Chairman Jeff Miller on Monday said
acting Veterans Affairs Secretary Sloan Gibson is doing a "great job,"
but that a full-time replacement should come from outside the
department.
At Swearing-In, Ex-General Vows 'Inclusive' Egypt
The New York Times / June 8, 2014 / By David Kirkpatrick
CAIRO - Abdel Fattah el-Sisi, the former general who led the military
takeover here nearly one year ago, was sworn in as president on Sunday,
testing the bet that a new strongman can overcome the economic
dysfunction and political polarization that bedeviled Egypt's three-year
experiment with democracy.
