December 24, 2014
Dear
Friend:
It has been my greatest
honor to represent you in 2014 which was a remarkable year. Our nation and
local community have made much progress, especially in promoting
opportunities in the 13th Congressional District of New
York.
With your support, I have been able to:
Hold
Tele-Town Hall
meetings with
over 11,500 constituents to address questions about affordable housing,
infrastructure, immigration reform, social security, unemployment
insurance benefits, and Earned Income Tax
Credit.
Hold meaningful rallies at the Capitol for
Immigration Reform
where I, along with 200 other
peaceful demonstrators, was arrested.
Highlight
important issues for the
community such as an increase in the minimum wage for airport workers and
for an increase in the
federal minimum
wage .
Provide resources and relief
efforts to those impacted by the tragic explosion that occurred in
East Harlem March 2014.
Provide town hall meetings to New York's 13th Congressional District
on
Health Care resources for New Yorkers , the
Affordable Care Act , and the
Basic Health
Program.
Engage
nearly 7,400 seniors to discuss issues ranging from the Affordable Care
Act, food stamps, immigration reform, to raising the federal minimum wage
and unemployment insurance benefits.
Passed my bill
that would rename an
Inwood post office building to honor
Corporal Juan M.
Alcntara (Pea)
who fought and died valiantly in Iraq in
2007.
Host
Career
Fairs ,
Tech Expos
and Talks to foster
innovation and entrepreneurialism, social security fairs ,
discussions on
Black History Month,
and also host
Congressional Art Competitions for
High School Students
in
New York.
Provide
webinars to teach residents how to effectively apply and compete for
federal jobs .
Introduce the
Renters Tax Credit Act of 2014
that proposes providing tax relief for low-income
tenants.
Contribute to innovative
technological movements within Harlem with the " Silicon
Harlem " tech conference.
Host women's empowerment events
to
commemorate
Womens History
Month .
To learn about more of our
accomplishments, please visit my website at at
www.rangel.house.gov
It has been a privilege to represent you again in the 114th
Congress.
In 2015, I will continue to fight for issues that you
care about, such as immigration reform, living wages, affordable housing,
healthcare, employment, veteran's issues, equality, poverty, social
security, civil Rights, education and foreign policy matters.
Your opinion and feedback are
important so please engage with me on
Facebook .com/CBRangel and also on
Twitter @cbrangel.
My best wishes and sincere gratitude to you and your
families for a joyful holiday season and a prosperous happy new
year.
Sincerely
Yours,
Congressman Charles B. Rangel
Please forward my
e-newsletter to your friends &amp; family .
