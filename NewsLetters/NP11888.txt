A group of Postmasters from Maine stopped by to discuss postal reform.
It was a delight to meet with Glenn Ross who I appointed Penobscot
County Sheriff when I was Governor.
It's always great to catch up with visiting Mainers at our weekly
Capitol Coffee.
Steve and Kelley Curtis of Stacyville stopped by from the Maine Chapter
of the Enlisted Association of the United States.
Constituent Services Representative Sarah Graettinger meets with
members of the Disabled Americans Veterans (DAV) Chapter 10 in Presque
Isle.
Dan Reardon, a member of my staff in Maine, meets with Maria Dorn,
Executive Director of the West Book Community Center, during our "Your
Government Your Neighborhood"
outreach
hours.
Impressions from the Middle East
This February I joined my colleague Senator Tim Kaine (D-VA) on a trip
to Israel, the West Bank, and Lebanon. During our time overseas, we had
productive meetings with our highly impressive embassy staff, foreign
dignitaries, military leaders, and individuals working to provide the
region with humanitarian assistance. You can read more about our trip
here.
Senator Tim Kaine and I tour an Iron Dome missile defense site near the
Gaza border.
Senator Tim Kaine and I pay our respects to Americans who have lost
their lives serving in Lebanon.
Senator Tim Kaine and I with Israeli President Benjamin Netanyahu.
Senator Tim Kaine and I meeting with Palestinian Authority President
Mahmoud Abbas.
Policy Update
*
At the beginning of February, the Senate passed the 2013 Farm
Bill
, important legislation that will define our nation's
agriculture policy for the next five years.
*
I teamed up with my colleague Sen. Deb Fischer (R-NB) to
introduce the PROTECT Act
, a bill that would protect jobs and prevent overregulation in the
health IT industry.
*	Enacting common-sense regulatory reform to promote innovation
and economic growth is one my priorities as a U.S. Senator. To help
inform my work, I'm asking people to share their experiences
with overly burdensome,
outdated, or duplicative federal regulations.
