In todays world, there is a lot of information that is critical to you and your loved ones well-being
financial records, healthcare information, Social Security, Medicare, TRICARE, and veteran's benefits to name a few. One way to protect yourself and your information is to take active precautions against identity theft. A few simple action steps can go a long way in protecting yourself; below are a few ideas I have found helpful:
Tips on Avoiding Identity Theft
o
Opt-out of pre-screened credit card offers at 1-888-5-OPTOUT.
o
Order a free annual credit report at
www.annualcreditreport.com
or 1-877-322-8228.
o
Add your name to the National Do Not Call Registry:
www.donotcall.gov
or 1-888-382-1222.
o
Dont carry your Social Security card.
o
Pay attention to what shows up on your bills.
o
Secure or shred your mail.
o
Dont give out personal information over the phone, through the mail or on the Internet unless youve initiated the contact or are sure you know who youre dealing with.
Yours in Service,
Randy
