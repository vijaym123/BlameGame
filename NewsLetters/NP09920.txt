2014 Congressional Art Competition Winners
From left to right: Jazmin Saunders and Rep Paul Broun
On Saturday, May 3rd, U.S. Representative Paul Broun unveiled the winning entry for the 2014 Congressional Art Competition. During a district ceremony at Georgia Square Mall, Congressman Broun announced Jazmin Saunders, a student at Walnut Grove High School, as the first place winner of the competition. Nateisha Anderson from Baldwin High School won 2nd Place, and Ashley Young from Piedmont Academy won 3rd Place. Union Grove High School students Alyson Smith and Analia Briceno-Gutierrez received Honorable Mentions.
Jazmin pictured with her winning artwork
Jazmin's oil painting, entitled,
Life After Death , will be on display in the hallways of the U.S. Capitol for an entire year for all visitors to see. Jazmin also received a gift card from a teen apparel store, an invitation to attend a national reception, and two airline tickets to Washington, DC. Congratulations Jazmin!

Interested in a Congressional Internship?
Year-round, Congressman Broun offers college students the opportunity to work in a Congressional office. This experience provides students with firsthand knowledge on how a Congressional office provides constituent services, interacts with the community, and how the legislative process works.
Currently, our offices in Washington, DC, and Athens, GA are accepting resumes for our summer internship program. Internships are unpaid, however, interns in any office can arrange to receive academic credit for their work. My internship program is open to all area college students, regardless of major. Candidates interested in a summer internship in Washington, DC can send their resume to Casey.Horgan@mail.house.gov and Andrew.Thorne@mail.house.gov for an internship in Athens, Georgia.
