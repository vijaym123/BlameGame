Beware Scammers Posing as the IRS
I continue to occasionally hear from constituents who receive phone calls from scammers claiming to be from the IRS. These scams typically start with claims that you have unpaid taxes and could be subject to fines and imprisonment. Sometimes, the scammers threaten to bring in the police. The IRS will never demand immediate payment over the phone and should never threaten legal action at the first contact. You can always verify that you owe nothing by calling the IRS directly, 1-800-829-1040. As always, feel free to contact my office if you have questions, 717-393-0667. For tips on recognizing scams,

Praying for Ebola-Stricken Nations
On Wednesday, I joined with community and faith leaders at St. James Episcopal Church in Lancaster to pray for people and nations suffering in the Ebola epidemic. Liberian ambassador to the U.S. Jeremiah Sulunteh was present and spoke of his countrys struggle. Liberia only emerged from a brutal civil war recently. While prosperity had grown, the nation still did not have a health system sufficient to deal with the crisis. Ebola must be stopped in Africa for the safety of the entire world. We need to take proper measures to keep it out of the U.S., and we must keep it from spilling over borders in Africa.
Halloween a Sweat Deal for Sugar Industry
This year, I fought very hard to get real reform of the USDA sugar program, which artificially inflates the price of sugar to protect the profits of growers and producers. This program also uses taxpayer-backed loans to cover industry losses, resulting in a hundreds of millions of dollars in payments within the last year. Op-ed of the Week: Some Things Are Truly Frightening
The problems we face as a nation are frightening. But right now, in many ways, we are like the person in the horror movie who is hiding under a bed. The thing is though, these problems are stalking us and will, sooner or later, they are going to drag us out from cover. Its far better to face them now, head on, then to wait until we have almost no options.
