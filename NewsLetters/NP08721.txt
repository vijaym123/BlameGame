
June 13, 2014
WASHINGTON, D.C . - In a continued effort to bring news and relevant information about our federal government back to his home district in Los Angeles, Representative Xavier Becerra (CA-34), Chairman of the House Democratic Caucus and Ranking Member of the Social Security Subcommittee, brings you The Becerra Bulletin, an e-newsletter filled with facts and information meant to stimulate thought and discussion about policy and a host of other issues.
BY THE NUMBERS - REBUILDING AMERICA'S INFRASTRUCTURE
It's time to Rebuild America and create jobs by fixing our crumbling roads, bridges and ports. Here's a by the numbers look at why Congress must act to reauthorize transportation funding to rebuild our country's infrastructure:
If Congress doesn't act, nearly
700,000 jobs
and
112,000 construction projects
are at risk
In Los Angeles County alone there are
1,297 deficient bridges
in need of repair
65% of America's major roads
are rated in less than good condition
25% of America's bridges
require significant repair or can't handle today's traffic
45% of Americans
lack access to transit

PURPLE LINE EXTENSION RECEIVES
1.25 BILLION IN FEDERAL FUNDING
On May 21, Congressman Becerra and federal officials announced that the
Federal Transit Administration (FTA) committed
$1.25 billion in funding for Metro's Purple Line Extension. When completed, the project will extend the Metro Purple Line subway nine miles west and add seven new stations.

SHORT TAKES - IN THE 34TH DISTRICT
2014 STUDENT ART COMPETITION
On May 14, Congressman Becerra recognized the winners of the 2014 Congressional Art Competition at a ceremony at Plaza de la Raza in Lincoln Heights. Benjamin Hunter, a senior at Ribet Academy took first place for his piece "Man on the Street/Home." Benjamin will receive a trip to Washington, D.C. to see his work on display in the halls of the United States Capitol where it will hang for one year.

FIGHTING SOCIAL SECURITY FRAUD
Congressman Becerra recently visited a local Social Security fraud-busting team (Cooperative Disability Unit, or CDI) in Los Angeles. Congressman Becerra has introduced legislation (H.R. 4090) which would expand Social Securitys ability to investigate and punish fraud and ensure that every state has a CDI unit.
