December 22, 2013
Rep. Kinzinger's
week in pictures
Congressman Kinzinger racked up the miles this
week travelling throughout the Sixteenth District to meet with
constituents, tour small businesses and nonprofit organizations, and host an
array of events. Continue reading below for pictures from his
week.
Nonprofit Agencies Tour
Rep. Kinzinger toured
nonprofit agencies throughout the district on Monday to hear about
the challenges they face in carrying out their missions and to highlight
the great work they do to meet the needs of struggling families this
season.
Hearing about types of nutrition assistance provided at We
Care of Grundy
County
Meeting with Sherry Pitney, Executive
Director of the Rockford Rescue
Mission
Touring the Salvation Army DeKalb Corps Community
Center
OSF Saint Anthony flight
In
Rockford, Rep. Kinzinger had the chance to fly in the OSF Saint Anthony
Medical Center Lifeline helicopter, an asset that allows critically
wounded or ill patients to be flown to the hospital for better
care.
Flight paramedics and crew of the Lifeline
helicopter
Gateway Services
At Gateway Services in
Princeton, Rep. Kinzinger was able to hear about the organization's
efforts to help individuals with developmental or physical disabilities
lead fuller, more independent lives in the community.
Visiting Gateway Services in
Princeton
Agriculture Advisory
Committee
Rep. Kinzinger kicked off the new Sixteenth District
Agriculture Advisory Committee, which will allow Sixteenth District farmers
to take their concerns on Ag policy directly to Adam.
First meeting of the AG Advisory
Committee at Ottawa City
Hall
Thanking Mike Sutfin, Ottawa City Flood Plain
Manager
Rep. Kinzinger had the chance to present Mike Sutfin with a
Congressional Record statement recognizing his work during springtime
flooding.
