Maya Angelou, Lyrical Witness of the Jim Crow South, Dies at 86
The New York Times / May 28, 2014 / By Margalit Fox
Maya Angelou, the memoirist and poet whose landmark book of 1969, "I
Know Why the Caged Bird Sings" - which describes in lyrical, unsparing
prose her childhood in the Jim Crow South - was among the first
autobiographies by a 20th-century black woman to reach a wide general
readership, died on Wednesday in her home.
Report: Nashville banks healthier than state average
Nashville Business Journal
/ May 27, 2014 / By Scott Harrison
Statewide, asset quality among Tennessee banks improved in the first
quarter. And though banks in the Nashville region experienced a slight
increase in the nonperforming asset ratio among their banks during the
quarter, lenders in the Greater Nashville area continued to have the
best asset quality in the state.
Thompson's Station grows the un-Wal-Mart way
The Tennessean / May 17, 2014 / By Jamie Page
Once known for growing prize-winning cattle, Thompson's Station is now
known for simply growing. With 4.9 percent growth, the town boasted the
highest population rise in Middle Tennessee in 2012 U.S. Census
estimates.
Syrians Abroad Vote in Advance of National Ballot
The New York Times / May 28, 2014 / By Anne Barnard and Alan Cowell
BEIRUT, Lebanon - Thousands of Syrians clogged approach roads and formed
lines outside their embassy here on Wednesday as voting for expatriates
began in an election that is expected to return President Bashar
al-Assad to power.
U.S. Troops to Leave Afghanistan by End of 2016
The New York Times / May 27, 2014 / By Mark Landler
WASHINGTON - President Obama, declaring that it was "time to turn the
page on a decade in which so much of our foreign policy was focused on
the wars in Afghanistan and Iraq," announced on Tuesday that he planned
to withdraw the last American troops from Afghanistan by the end of
2016.
Brothers mark 35 years with kidney gift
The Tennessean / May 27, 2014 / By Tom Wilemon
For 35 years, Tom Cooper has pondered how to say thank you on the
anniversary of the day his little brother saved his life.
Rebelling Against Abuse, Afghan Women See Signs of Change
The New York Times / May 27, 2014 / By Alissa Rubin
PUL-I-KUMRI, Afghanistan - Zahra said a neighbor raped her in her home
on Friday. It was the most humiliating event in her unremittingly
painful life, and the next day she begged her husband, Najibullah, to
move their family so the man could not attack her again. He refused.
Artist shares life lessons with Metro students
The Tennessean / May 27, 2014 / By Mignonne Bryant
In a Wright Middle School classroom, Ashshahid Muhammad - a student at
the Nossi College of Art - teaches middle schoolers how to draw an eye.
Google's new driverless car has no brakes or steering wheel
Washington Post / May 28, 2014 / By Gail Sullivan
For the past few years, Google has been test-driving Lexus SUVs
retrofitted with self-driving technology. On Tuesday, the company
announced it would test the technology in its own cars. It also gave a
preview to reporters gathered for a conference in California.
Titans' Locker Returns After Season-Ending Injury
News Channel 5 / May 28, 2014 / By Associated Press
NASHVILLE, Tenn. (AP) - Jake Locker is back on the field with the
Tennessee Titans - a couple of weeks ahead of the schedule doctors
estimated for his return from an injured right foot.
