This week, the House of Representatives approved the
Financial Services and Government Services Appropriations Act, HR 5106,
which takes several steps to limit IRS abuse and
overreach.
Most importantly, this bill
prevents the IRS from enforcing
Obamacare
by prohibiting any funds from being used to enforce
the individual mandate.

The bill
also:
1)
Cuts IRS funding
by $1.48 billion below last year, bringing total IRS funding
below 2003 levels .
2)
Prohibits IRS targeting
by blocking any funds from being used to
subject organizations to additional scrutiny based on political beliefs,
or to target individuals for exercising their First Amendment
rights.
3)
Stops waste
by prohibiting
funding for bonuses (unless tax compliance and employee performance
are considered), frivolous conferences and inappropriate
videos.
4)
Prevents destruction of IRS
records
by prohibiting any funding from being used to destroy or dispose of
records in contravention with Federal Records
Act.
5)
Protects taxpayers
by require the IRS to
institute and enforce policies to safeguard taxpayer information, and to
establish an employee training program that covers issues like
taxpayers rights, ethics, and applying tax law
impartially.
