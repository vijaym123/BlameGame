Forbes Receives Distinguished Service Award from
VA Farm Bureau
Washing, D.C.
- Congressman J. Randy Forbes (VA-04) has been presented with the Virginia Farm Bureau Federations (VFBF) 2013 Distinguished Service Award at the VFBF Annual Convention in Richmond. Forbes was singled out for his strong support of farmers and agricultural issues in Southeast Virginia, as evidenced by his legislative record.
From cotton to corn to peanuts, producers in Congressman Forbes district can count on him to listen to their concerns and do all he can to solve their problems at the federal level, said VFBF President Wayne F. Pryor.
With an economic impact of over $50 billion annually, agriculture is Virginia's largest industry, Congressman Forbes said, providing more than 357,000 jobs in the Commonwealth. Im proud to be able to fight on behalf of the hardworking farmers of the Fourth District, and remain committed to ensuring that farming remains an important pillar of our economy both in Virginia, and across the nation.
The Farm Bureau is an independent, non-governmental, voluntary organization governed by and representing farm and ranch families united for the purpose of analyzing their problems and formulating action to achieve educational improvement, economic opportunity and social advancement and, thereby, to promote the national well-being.
The Farm Bureau is local, county, state, national and international in its scope and influence and is non-partisan, non-sectarian and non-secret in character.
