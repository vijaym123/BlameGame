BEATTY PASSES FIRST BILL IN U.S. HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
A long-time advocate to end human trafficking, Rep. Beattys legislation to reduce the instances and impacts of child sex trafficking passed the U.S. House of Representatives on July 24, 2014. The bill, H.R. 5111, would help victims of child sex trafficking by de-criminalizing their behavior and making it easier for people to report potential instances to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children Cyber Tipline. FINANCIAL SERVICES AND
HOUSING ROUNDTABLE
Over fifty financial services, nonprofit, and government representatives filled a meeting room at the Columbus Metropolitan Housing Authoritys Jenkins Terrace on May 14th to attend a Financial Services and Housing Briefing and Panel hosted by Congresswoman Beatty. Rep. Beatty was joined at the event by her Congressional colleague Rep. Al Green, TX-09, Ranking
Member of the Committee on Financial Services Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations.
Sitting on the Financial Services Committee in the U.S. House of Representatives, these two
Members are uniquely positioned to tackle ongoing challenges with the housing
recovery and ensuring an accessible housing and rental stock for all Americans.
WOMEN LEADERS COME TO D.C.
On April 28, 2014, Congresswoman Beatty hosted 14 women leaders from Ohios Third Congressional District in Washington on a day long Women In Leadership Fly-In. The group spent the day meeting with senior Congressional advisors, elected officials from both political parties, and other women in senior positions in Washington.
Throughout the day, the group engaged in a dialogue about a range of policy issues, including equal pay for equal work, paid sick leave, and early childhood education. The visit also included a meeting with senior White House staff in the Roosevelt Room, just steps away from the Oval Office.
BEATTY HELPS BRING $30 MILLION TO DISTRICT
Congresswoman Beatty joined with HUD Secretary Shaun Donovan, Columbus Mayor Michael Coleman, CMHA President and CEO Charles Hillman, and OSU President Dr. Michael Drake on June 30 to announce $227 million in urban redevelopment funds for Near East Side, led by an initial $30 million in HUD grant funding. Beatty partnered with the Columbus Metropolitan Housing Authority, The Ohio State University, and Columbus City Mayor Michael Coleman to push this forward this important project.
