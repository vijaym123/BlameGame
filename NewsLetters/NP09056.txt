Today's trivia question: Which president gave the shortest Inaugural
speech at just 135 words?
Domestic violence in Tennessee drops 5.7 percent
The Tennessean / June 3, 2014/ By Brian Haas and Adam Tamburin
Tennessee has made more progress in its effort to shed its image as one
of the worst states in the nation for domestic violence.
Numbers show how bad state's pill popping is
The Tennessean / June 3, 2014 / By Tom Wilemon
In a state where one out of every 20 people pops a pain pill for
recreational use, the consequences are deadly.
Dollar General Expanding As Primary Rival Shrinks
WPLN / June 3, 2014 / By Bradley George
Goodlettsville-based Dollar General already has more than 11,000 stores
in 40 states. In 2015, the discount retailer will open stores in Oregon,
Rhode Island and Maine for the first time.
Crime at Tennessee schools continues decline, TBI says
The Tennessean / June 3, 2014 / By Josh Brown and Maranda Faris
Crime fell at Tennessee schools last year for at least the sixth year in
a row, according to a report released Tuesday.
Nashville disputes No. 3 rank in teacher absences
The Tennessean / June 3, 2014 / By Joey Garrison
A new study says Metro Nashville Public Schools rank third nationally in
classroom absences accumulated by teachers, but MNPS officials are
questioning its methods and rejecting the findings.
Tennessee DCS 'back on track,' court monitors say
The Tennessean / June 3, 2014 / By Tony Gonzalez
Tennessee's child welfare system rebounded under new leadership in the
past year - although challenges remain - experts say in a new 505-page
report.
Acadia buying UK's second-largest behavioral health provider for $660
million
Nashville Business Journal / June 3, 2014 / By Eleanor Kennedy
Acadia Healthcare Company has signed a definitive agreement to buy
Partnerships in Care, the second largest behavioral health provider in
the United Kingdom, for $660 million in cash, according to a news
release.
Forums scheduled for Metro school board races
The Tennessean / June 3, 2014 / By Joey Garrison
The Nashville Area Chamber of Commerce, in partnership with the Urban
League of Middle Tennessee and the Metropolitan Nashville Education
Association, have organized four forums starting next week on candidates
running for Metro school board.
Nashville a top large city for job growth
Nashville Business Journal
/ June 4, 2014 / By Brandon Gee
The Nashville metropolitan area is the sixth best large city in the
nation for job growth. Nashville comes in 24th place when small and
mid-sized cities are included, too.
In Show of Support, Obama Meets With Ukraine Leader
The New York Times / June 4, 2014 / By Peter Baker
WARSAW - President Obama met on Wednesday for the first time with the
newly elected president of Ukraine and pledged American support for
efforts to stabilize a country torn by corruption, economic turmoil and
a violent pro-Russian insurgency broadly believed to be fomented by
Moscow.
