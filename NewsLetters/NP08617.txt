Today's trivia question: Who were the first (and only) father and son to
serve in the Senate simultaneously?
Tennessee faces epidemic of drug-dependent babies
The Tennessean
/ June 13, 2014 / By Shelley DuBois and Tony Gonzalez
No one who hears it ever forgets the sound.
There were no cool heads when they were needed
The Tennessean / June 15, 2014 / By Frank Daniels III
Our problems with domestic violence and abuse are bad enough without the
system cooperating to circumvent the circuit breakers that have proved
to work.
Artificial Pancreas Shows Promise in Diabetes Test
The New York Times / June 15, 2014 / By Nicholas Bakalar
A portable artificial pancreas built with a modified iPhone successfully
regulated blood sugar levels in a trial with people who have Type 1
diabetes, researchers reported Sunday.
Israel Demands Palestinian Leader's Help After Abductions
The New York Times / June 16, 2014 / By Jodi Rudoren
JERUSALEM - Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu of Israel told President
Mahmoud Abbas of the Palestinian Authority on Monday that he expected
his help in finding three kidnapped Israeli teenagers and capturing
their abductors.
Gazprom Stops Supplying Natural Gas to Ukraine
The New York Times / June 16, 2014 / By Neil MacFarquhar
MOSCOW - The Russian energy giant Gazprom stopped supplying natural gas
to Ukraine on Monday, warning that the reduction could diminish the
amount of gas flowing to the rest of Europe and escalating the political
crisis between the two countries.
Starbucks to Provide Free College Education to Thousands of Workers
The New York Times / June 15, 2014 / By Richard Perez-Pena
Starbucks will provide a free online college education to thousands of
its workers, without requiring that they remain with the company,
through an unusual arrangement with Arizona State University, the
company and the university will announce on Monday.
U.S. and Iran Hint at Iraq Talks as Rebels Seize New City
The New York Times / June 16, 2014 / By Rod Nordland and Thomas Erdbrink
BAGHDAD - Iraqi extremists took over the small city of Tal Afar, in
northwestern Iraq, on Monday, according to Iraqi security officials and
residents, sending both Shiite and Sunni residents fleeing.
As Massacre Claim Shakes Iraq, Militants Seize New City
The New York Times / June 15, 2014 / By Rod Nordland and Alissa Rubin
BAGHDAD - Wielding the threat of sectarian slaughter, Sunni Islamist
militants claimed on Sunday that they had massacred hundreds of captive
Shiite members of Iraq's security forces, posting grisly pictures of a
mass execution in Tikrit as evidence and warning of more killing to
come.
Elton John closes 13th Bonnaroo with huge crowd
The Tennessean / June 16, 2014 / By Dave Paulson
Just how many people are at Bonnaroo this year? That question has been
hard to get a handle on -- especially because no single performer in the
festival's first three days appeared to really bring out all of the
attendees en masse.
Second Flags of Honor wraps up in Mt. Juliet
The Tennessean / June 16, 2014 / By Andy Jumbles
Around 500 flags were sold over the weekend for the second Flags of
Honor event, held by two civic organizations in Mt. Juliet.
Answer: Henry and Augustus Dodge of Wisconsin and Iowa, respectively.
