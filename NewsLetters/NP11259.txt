Two Spring Opportunities for Georgia High School
Students
Every year, Members of Congress sponsor the
annual Congressional Art Competition.
This year, we're introducing
the first ever House App Challenge as well for those students who
excel in the sciences.
Learn more about both competitions here, and
contact my district office at 770-297-3388 if you have any
questions!
Congressional Art Competition
The
Congressional Art Competition is open to public, private, and home-educated
high school students across the Ninth District.
The winning piece
will be displayed in the collection of 2014 winners at the U.S.
Capitol, and the artist will have the opportunity to present their piece to
Congressman Collins.
Guidelines for
artwork:
Artwork must be original and in any of the
following mediums:
-- Paintings: oil, acrylics, watercolor,
etc.
-- Drawings: colored pencil, pencil, ink, marker, pastels,
charcoal
-- Collages: must be two dimensional
-- Mixed Media: use
of more than two mediums (i.e. pencil, ink, watercolor, etc)
--
Computer-generated art
-- Photographs
Pieces should
be no larger than 28x28x4 (including frame) and should not exceed
15 pounds in weight
Each entry must be original in
concept, design, and execution and may not violate U.S. copyright laws.
Note: entries copied from an existing photo or image that was created by
someone other than the student is a violation of competition rules and
will not be accepted.
Submissions should be delivered to
Congressman Collins Gainesville Office by
5:00 PM on May
5 th .
Students and art
teachers may learn more by visiting
http://dougcollins.house.gov/art-competition
and by contacting Tena Riley in Congressman
Collins Gainesville office at
770-297-3388.
House App
Challenge
This year marks the first annual Congressional Science, Technology,
Engineering and Math (STEM) Academic Competition, the House App
Contest.
This new competition is designed to engage students
creativity and encourage their participation in STEM education
fields.
The House App Challenge is open to all high school
students in Georgias 9 th
District.
Established by Members
of the U.S. House of Representatives in 2013, this competition is a
nationwide event that allows high school students from across the
country to compete by creating and exhibiting their software application, or
app, for mobile, tablet, or computer devices on a platform of their
choice.
Students entering the competition must
submit their apps source code online during the Competition Submission
Period between
by
11:59 PM Eastern Daylight Time on APRIL
30TH, 2014,
as well as provide a YouTube or VIMEO video demo
explaining their app and what they learned through this competition
process.
Students and teachers may learn more by visiting
http://dougcollins.house.gov/stem-competition
and by
calling Darren Kendall in Congressman Collins Gainesville office at
770-297-3388.
